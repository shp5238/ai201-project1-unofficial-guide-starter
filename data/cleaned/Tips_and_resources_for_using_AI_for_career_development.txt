Tips and resources for using Generative AI for Career
Development – USD Career Development Center

The world of work is evolving faster than ever, and new technologies are reshaping how
we explore, learn, connect, and prepare for our futures. This guide offers practical
resources and tips to help you leverage Generative AI ethically, thoughtfully, and
effectively as you explore career options, build skills, and prepare and embark on your
career endeavors. The AI tools and guides provided are powerful resources designed
for skill development and content support. They are here to help you explore, refine,
and research; they are not a replacement for your personal effort, work, critical thinking,
or the professional advice of our Career Development Center staff.

output. You are solely responsible for verifying all information, editing all content,

1.​ Why Use AI in Your Career Journey?
●​ Enhance your preparation: AI can help you practice, get feedback, and
sharpen your career journey.
●​ Time saving: efficiency and scalability
●​ Tapping into the vast data that AI is ready to synthesize: For example, job
market and industry research, idea generation, etc.
●​ Simulation of different audiences: recruiters, hiring managers, Applicant
Tracking Systems (ATS), etc.
●​ Skill gap identification and personalized recommendations
●​ Language and writing support. AI can help you edit and polish your
application materials, such as your resume and cover letter, and offer
feedback on tone, clarity, and structure.

2.​ How can AI help me in my job search?
●​ Resume and cover letter editing and tailoring
●​ Assistance with other job search correspondence, such as thank-you
notes and networking communications.
●​ Interview preparation, mock interview Q&As

●​ Career exploration and industry/job market research

3.​ Limitations, Ethics, and Things to Consider
●​ AI has biases and makes mistakes
●​ Privacy and data security
●​ Over-reliance or loss of authenticity
●​ Academic integrity and professional ethics
●​ Critical thinking is still essential – AI should complement, not replace, your
judgment, creativity, and own voice
●​ Organization policies about applicants using AI

4.​ General Tips​

●​ Be specific in your prompts:
AI tools are trained on massive datasets and can provide highly tailored
insights—but only if you’re clear and detailed in your request. Include your
goals, audience, tone, and relevant background.​

●​ Use your own voice:
AI can assist with structure, grammar, and clarity, but your writing should
still reflect your authentic tone, values, and personality—especially in
cover letters, resumes, correspondence, or interview prep.​

●​ Iterative Prompting:
You don’t have to get everything right in one try. Ask follow-up questions
like: “Can you make this more concise?,” or “Refine this in a more
conversational tone.”​

●​ Always carefully review and edit AI responses:
AI tools are language models that can sometimes make factual errors
(“hallucinations”) and false information, insert clichés, or overlook
nuances. Always double-check outputs for accuracy. ​

●​ Protect your privacy:
Don’t share your full name, phone number, or any other personally
identifying information in open or public versions of AI tools.​

●​ Research organization policies:

When applying to internships, jobs, or other career opportunities be sure
to research whether the organization has any policies in regards to
applicants utilizing AI.

●​ Seek human opinion:
AI is a powerful tool for editing and brainstorming, but it does not fully
replace the sentiment and perception of a human reader. After using AI,
consider bringing your draft to a career counselor, faculty mentor, or
writing tutor for feedback.

●​ Do the work:
AI can help you proactively enhance your career development, however it
is not meant to replace you investing the time and effort to do your own
work. Leverage AI as an optimization tool rather than replacing your efforts
and voice. You are responsible for any application materials you submit or
correspondence you send.

Sample Prompt 1: Resume editing request

Tips: This is an example of a quick, one-off resume editing prompt. Even in short
prompts, be sure to include three key elements:
1.​ A brief summary of your background
2.​ The intended audience or employer/industry
3.​ The purpose of the resume (e.g., internship, full-time role, job fair, networking)
While AI tools draw from massive datasets and can offer helpful guidance, the more
specific your prompt is, the more accurate and relevant the response will be.

Example:
I am a third year Accounting major applying for summer internships with the Big4
accounting firms. This is a resume for the accounting recruitment fair, and I
would like you to advise me from the perspective of a college recruiter from a big
accounting firm.

Sample Prompt 2: Resume editing request – use delimiters to create a reusable
template

Tips: ###s (delimiters) help structure your prompt by clearly labeling different sections,
such as ###My Background or ###Target Audience. While optional, these markers
make your prompt easier for AI to interpret and respond to accurately. They also allow
you to build reusable templates for common tasks, such as resume reviews or cover
letter drafts. Using clear delimiters improves clarity, especially when your prompt
includes multiple parts or detailed instructions.

Example:
###Who Am I: I am a sophomore Electrical Engineering major

###What Am I Doing: Applying for an Electrical Engineering internship position at
ABCcom

###My Audience: ABCcom college recruiter and likely Applicant Tracking System
(ATS) screening

###AI Perspective: Assume the perspective of an experienced college recruiter from a
large tech company such as ABCcom

###Specific Goals: Besides matching keywords and key skills, highlight my
transferable experience since I haven’t had internships before

###Job Description: [copy/paste job description, especially the responsibilities and
requirements/qualifications]

###My Resume: [copy/paste the content of your resume. Omit your name, contact
information and other personal information]

Sample Prompt 3: Resume Summary/Profile creation

Tips: If you have extensive work experience, adding a “Summary” section at the top of
your resume can help highlight your most relevant skills and qualifications—especially
those aligned with the job you’re targeting.
When asking AI to help you write this summary, be sure to provide:
●​ The format you want (e.g., brief paragraph, bullet points, or a combination)
●​ The desired length or word count

●​ The job description and key qualifications for the role you're applying to
The more context you give, the more tailored and effective the summary will be.

sections, such as ###My Background or ###Target Audience. While optional, these

or cover letter drafts. Using clear delimiters improves clarity, especially when your

###Intention: Create a brief, 80-word “Summary” section of my resume, highlighting
most relevant skills that match the specific job that I am applying for.

###Background: I have 10 years of experience in software development and now I am

[areas of relevant expertise, coursework, etc.].

requirements/qualifications]

Prompt Sample 4: Cover Letter Optimization

Tips: Similar to resume optimization, the AI can most effectively optimize your cover
letter if you provide specific background information such as who you are, the purpose
and the audience of the letter, and the specific company and position that you are
applying to. To maintain your own voice and writing style, it is best to provide your rough
draft in the prompt instead of asking AI to write the letter from scratch. Use AI as a
writing assistant, not the writer.

Delimiters: ###s (delimiters) help structure your prompt by clearly labeling different
sections, such as ###My Background or ###Target Audience. While optional, these
markers make your prompt easier for AI to interpret and respond to accurately. They
also allow you to build reusable templates for common tasks, such as resume reviews
or cover letter drafts. Using clear delimiters improves clarity, especially when your
prompt includes multiple parts or detailed instructions.

Example:
###Purpose: Optimizing my cover letter for my summer research application

###Background: I am a third year Biology major with prior experience conducting
research on campus. This is the first time I am applying for a research position with a
biotech company

###Perspective: Advise me as if you are a recruiter from a major biotech company,
especially with experience recruiting interns or entry level research positions.

###Tone and style: I want to sound personable and authentic, but still professionally
appropriate for the biotech industry. Please try to keep my original voice while providing
editing suggestions.

###Job Description: [copy/paste job description, especially the responsibilities and
requirements/qualifications]

###My Cover Letter: [copy/paste the draft of your cover letter. Omit name and personal

Prompt Sample 5: Interview Preparation

Tips: When using AI to help you prepare for an interview, be sure to include:
●​ Basic information about yourself, the employer, and the position
●​ The interview format (e.g., phone screen, panel, behavioral, technical)
●​ The type of questions or topics you want to practice
You can also tell the AI how you'd like to practice—for example:
●​ General interview tips
●​ Mock Q&A, one question at a time
●​ Feedback on your sample answers
The more specific you are, the more effective and tailored your practice session will be.

Delimiters: ###s (delimiters) help structure your prompt by clearly labeling different
sections, such as ###My Background or ###Target Audience. While optional, these
markers make your prompt easier for AI to interpret and respond to accurately. They
also allow you to build reusable templates for common tasks, such as resume reviews
or cover letter drafts. Using clear delimiters improves clarity, especially when your
prompt includes multiple parts or detailed instructions.

Example:
###About me: graduating senior, Architecture major, [summarize relevant skills], had
one internship with a construction company prior.

###About the Organization: XYZ company, a construction and renovation contractor
specializing in hospitals and office buildings.

###The position: Project Coordinator position in the Renovation department.

###Format: This is a second round, in-person interview with a panel of 4 interviewers.

###Audience: The panel includes the hiring manager (Project Manager), a team
member from the same department (Project Coordinator), the department head (VP of
Project Operations), and an HR specialist.

###Perspective: When coaching and advising me, please prioritize the perspective of
the hiring manager.

###Practice: I would like to practice behavioral interview questions and STAR
storytelling. Please read the following job descriptions and come up with 3 likely
behavioral questions. Let’s practice one question at a time – you ask, I answer, you
critique, and repeat.

###Job Description: [copy/paste job description, especially the responsibilities and
requirements/qualifications]

Prompt Sample 6: Career exploration and industry research

Tips: Career exploration can feel open-ended, and you may not always have a specific
job title or description in mind. To make your conversation with AI more focused and
useful, start by sharing some key information about yourself, such as:
●​ Background (e.g., major, experience)
●​ Your interests, skills, values, and needs
●​ Any specific preferences (e.g., location, work-life balance, further education,
immediate transition or long-term planning)

Then, clarify what you want AI to help with. For example:
●​ Identifying job roles that match your current skills
●​ Exploring industries aligned with your interests and values
●​ Understanding skill gaps for a role or industry you’re curious about
The more direction you give, the more tailored and insightful the conversation will be.

Delimiters: ###s (delimiters) help structure your prompt by clearly labeling different
sections, such as ###My Background or ###Target Audience. While optional, these
markers make your prompt easier for AI to interpret and respond to accurately. They
also allow you to build reusable templates for common tasks, such as resume reviews
or cover letter drafts. Using clear delimiters improves clarity, especially when your
prompt includes multiple parts or detailed instructions.

Example:
###About me: A marketing major, senior, with some experience in social media
marketing through internships and student organization work. Now I am interested in
exploring careers in branding and product marketing, and how my previous experience
and marketing coursework can help me connect to my future career.

###Interest: I like working with people and I like teaching people new concepts. I would
like to explore where and how my skills may be valuable in marketing settings.

###Skills: I have done social media internships and know how to create content, run
campaigns, and look at analytics. I’m good at identifying trends and explaining ideas
clearly to different audiences.

###Values for an ideal job: I want a job where I can help build brands that actually
connect with people. Working with a team is really important to me, and I like places
that let you be creative and try new things. I also care about working somewhere that’s
honest, ethical, and values its people.

###Needs for an ideal job: Prefer West coast, [ideal salary range] to start, hybrid or
flexible work schedule, and opportunities for career growth. I am open to entry level
marketing jobs that help me build the right skills.

###Hope to learn: I am just starting to figure out what’s out there beyond social media
management. I want to explore different entry level roles in branding, product marketing,
or marketing strategy. Later, I can narrow it down to specific industries, companies, and
job titles.

###Perspective: Please converse with me as if you are an experienced career coach
who has lots of experience in the marketing field. Also please add perspective as a
corporate recruiter who recruits for related roles for new college grads.

Prompt Sample 7: Career exploration and industry research (early college)
Tips: Career exploration can feel open-ended, especially early in college, and you
might not have a clear idea of a specific job or career path yet. To make your
conversation with AI more useful, you can start by sharing a few things about yourself,
such as:
●​ Background: your major, any classes you enjoy, and any experiences so far
(part-time jobs, clubs, volunteer work, hobbies).
●​ Interests, skills, values, and needs: even if you’re not totally sure, mention what
excites you, what you enjoy doing, or what kind of environment you like.
●​ Preferences: things like location, work-life balance, the possibility of grad school,
or whether you want to focus on immediate opportunities versus long-term
planning.
Then, let AI know how you want it to help. For example, you could ask it to:
●​ Explore industries you might enjoy based on your values or hobbies.
●​ Identify skills you might want to develop if you’re curious about a field, without
needing to commit to a specific role yet.
●​ Or just have an open-ended conversation about how your skills and interests
may connect to different paths in the career world.
The more direction you give, the more tailored and insightful the conversation will be.

Delimiters: ###s (delimiters) help structure your prompt by clearly labeling different
sections, such as ###My Background or ###Target Audience. While optional, these
markers make your prompt easier for AI to interpret and respond to accurately. They
also allow you to build reusable templates for common tasks, such as resume reviews
or cover letter drafts. Using clear delimiters improves clarity, especially when your
prompt includes multiple parts or detailed instructions.

Example:
###About me: I thought I would be pre-med for years, but the more I think about med
school and being a doctor, the more doubt I have. I am doing ok in my bio/biochem
classes but I just don't enjoy them and I am not sure if I want to continue this path.

###Interest: I like helping people and solving problems. I have been playing sports

###Skills: I haven't done much work except hospital volunteering. I think I am good at
working with people and I am ok in science and labs too.

###Values for an ideal job: Helping people is essential. I would like to work in an

###Needs for an ideal job: Good pay and job security. I don't mind extra schooling to
get there. I like working hard and like to keep myself busy as long as my job keeps me
motivated.

###Hope to learn: I just started to consider anything outside pre-med recently. I would
like to chat about whether med-school/medicine is right for me, and what other careers
may be potential good matches to consider.

###Perspective: I would like to have a more conversational and interactive discussion
